  • The Electromagnetic Interaction is responsible for almost all macroscopic properties of matter, except the motion of objects subject to gravitational fields created by planetary size objects.  For example, such diverse phenomena as atomic spectra, friction and elasticity all have their origins rooted in electromagnetism.

  • At an introductory level electricty and magnetism are conveniently discussed as separate, but related, physical phenomena.  A single theory completely describing both phenomena was developed by James Clerk Maxwell at the end of the nineteenth century.  This was the first example of unification of apparently different forces of nature.


  • Since that time physicists have continued to develop a unifying theory of all the forces of nature - the so-called "Theory of Everything (ToE)".  There has been some success in this endeavour.  The "standard model" describes interactions of elementary particles due to the electromagnetic, weak and strong forces.  Only gravity remains to be included in a final (?) unified theory.
