  • These equations describe all of electromagnetism at the non-quantum level (macroscopic scale), known as classical electromagnetism.  This includes, for example, the phenomena of electric motors, generators, radio, TV, radar, microwaves, wave optics.
  • May be compared in importance in classical physics to Newton's Laws of Motion and Gravitation.
  • However, Newton's Laws turn out to be only an approximation in the limit of speeds significantly less than the speed of light.  They must be replaced by Einstein's theory of relativity in this regime.  Maxwell's equations have no such limitation - they are relativistically invariant - accurate at any speeds.
Written in the form above the equations are true only in a vacuum.  In polarizable and/or magnetizable media the permittivity and permeabilities of the vacuum are replaced by the permittivity and permeability of the medium.
